[L1] # How to re-encode video files for better compatibility on your devices

[L2] 

[L3] Amazon Fire TV, Android or Roku rely on Hardware decode to decode your videos files.

[L4] 

[L5] Most of the time it should be OK, however, there could be times when codec is not recognized or support by your devices.

[L6] 

[L7] For example, here's a link to Amazon's [supported codec](https://developer.amazon.com/docs/fire-tv/device-specifications.html) or [Roku supported codec.](https://developer.roku.com/en-gb/docs/specs/streaming.md)

[L8] 

[L9]  

[L10] 

[L11] To solve the problem you will need to convert/re-encode your video into something that Amazon, Android or Roku device support.

[L12] 

[L13] There's a [Handbreak is a free](https://handbrake.fr/) and very popular software to do that. It runs on Windows, Mac and Linux.

[L14] 

[L15] To re-encode your video.

[L16] 

[L17] Download, Run Handbreak.

[L18] 

[L19] Drag your video in there.

[L20] 

[L21] ![mceclip0.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/360055670394)

[L22] 

[L23] Select the output format you desired. Amazon, Android or Roku, depend on your device. Pick appropriate video resolution you want too.

[L24] 

[L25] ![mceclip0.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/360056534533)

[L26] 

[L27] Select a location you want the new video file to be output to.

[L28] 

[L29] Click Start.

[L30] 

[L31] ![mceclip2.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/360055670414)

[L32] 

[L33] Depend on video length and quality, and how fast your computer is. It could take some time.

[L34] 

[L35] When it's done, you can upload the file back to OptiSigns and assign to your playlists, screens.

[L36]
