[L1] # My image, content are bleeding off the screens with Fire TV (need screen calibration)

[L2] 

[L3] With Fire TV Stick 4K. You can calibrate the screens to avoid images, content bleeding off the screen.

[L4] 

[L5] If you are using Fire TV HD, it's not possible.  

[L6] You may want to look at your TV settings, some TV have this setting too.

[L7] 

[L8] ![stpuid-new-screen-calibration-message-fire-tv-stick-2](https://i2.wp.com/www.aftvnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/stpuid-new-screen-calibration-message-fire-tv-stick-2.jpg?resize=646%2C363&quality=100)

[L9] 

[L10] **How to 'Calibrate Your Display' to Fire TV Stick 4K?**  

[L11] **Step 1**  

[L12] On the **'Home screen'** window, look for the **'Settings'** tab in the options listed on the top bar.

[L13] 

[L14] **Step 2**  

[L15] Under the **'Settings'** tab, look for the **'Display & Sounds'** option among the list that will be displayed.

[L16] 

[L17] ![](https://i.imgur.com/HUhrL3B.jpg)**Step 3**  

[L18] In the **'Display & Sounds'** option, go to the **'Display'** option from the list of alternatives provided.

[L19] 

[L20] ![](https://i.imgur.com/6ZFxqEQ.jpg)**Step 4**  

[L21] In the **'Display'** tab, select the **'Calibrate Display'** option from the two alternatives provided.

[L22] 

[L23] **Step 5**  

[L24] You will be taken to the calibrator screen. As mentioned, start calibrating your device display using the **'UP'** and **'DOWN'** buttons provided on the remote, making the arrows completely visible. Select **'Accept'**.

[L25]
