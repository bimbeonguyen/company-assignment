[L34] ## Design Differences in Designer 2.0

[L35] 

[L36] ### Left-Aligned Side Menu

[L37] 

[L38] The biggest change for users will be the look and placement of functions in Designer 2.0. The new version streamlines the design, left-aligning a lot of the previous buttons and functions.¬†

[L39] 

[L40] Most functions are now relocated to the left-hand side.

[L42] ![Designer screenshot highlighting toolbar](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432408655379)

[L43] 

[L44] The left side menu will also replace the pop-up window that was utilized for certain functions.

[L45] 

[L46] The functions now in the left side menu include:¬†

[L47] 

[L48] * Position

[L49] * Layers, formerly called ‚ÄúObjects‚Äù

[L50] * Data Sources

[L51] * Animation

[L52] * Widget format options

[L53] * Settings

[L54] * Text

[L55] * And more

[L57] For example, Animations controls will now appear in the left side menu.

[L62] 

[L63] Effects is the new category for accessing visual effects. This includes some of our previous functions, such as Shadow, Outline, Texture, Gradient, and Opacity.

[L64] 

[L65] To access these functions:¬†

[L66]

[L68] * **Choose** any of the functions from the side menu

[L74] ### Zoom

[L75] 

[L76] We‚Äôve updated the navigation controls in Designer 2.0 to make moving around your design more efficient.

[L77] 

[L78] In Designer 2.0:

[L79] 

[L80] * Scroll to move up and down your design

[L81] * Press **CTRL + scroll** to zoom in and out

[L82] 

[L83] In Designer 1.0, scrolling would zoom in and out of the design, and you had to use scrollbars to navigate. In Designer 2.0, scrolling is now used for navigation, while CTRL + scroll controls zooming.

[L84] 

[L85] ### Layers

[L86] 

[L87] Similarly, Objects has been renamed to Layers and moved to the left-hand side menu. This allows you to edit the positioning or layering of any of the elements of your design.

[L88] 

[L89] To access Layers:

[L90] 

[L91] * Select the **layered icon** on the right side

[L92] * OR select **Position**

[L93] * Select **Layers**

[L94] 

[L95] Then you can select any of the elements and move them around to change their position or "layering."

[L96] 

[L97] ![Designer screenshot of purple design with a girl and white letters, arrows show how to use Layers](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432385843347)

[L98] 

[L99] ### History

[L100] 

[L101] This new feature allows you to track all your most recent changes to your design.¬†

[L102] 

[L103] Careful! Clicking on a previous ‚Äúevent‚Äù and starting to make changes to your design will erase any events¬† created after that specific event.¬†

[L104] 

[L105] To access History:¬†

[L106] 

[L107] * Select the **layered icon** on the right side

[L108] * OR select **Position** button from the t0ol bar

[L109] * **History** is the right option that will pop up in the side menu

[L110] * **Click** any event to move to that point in your editing

[L111] 

[L112] ![Designer screenshot of purple design with a girl and white letters, arrows show how to use History function](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432385843731)

[L114] ### Canvas Resize Options

[L115] 

[L116] We have added Landscape and Portrait options to the Resizing option for greater ease of use. This allows you to reshape your canvas and design with a click of a button. Made the design in landscape but you need it for a screen in portrait mode? No worries!

[L117] 

[L118] * Select **Resize**from the tool bar

[L119] * Deselect **Maintain Aspect Ratio**

[L120] * Select **Portrait** or **Landscape**

[L121] 

[L122] ![Designer screenshot featuring part of a purple design and arrows show how to change design orientation](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432408666643)

[L128] ### Reusable Component Library

[L129] 

[L130] This feature allows you to save any design element or grouped elements to a component library. Save any element you like and create a library of branded or themed elements, or simply one that you wanted to save for later!

[L131] 

[L132] To add the component:¬†

[L133] 

[L134] * Click on **the element** you would like to save

[L135] * Select **More**

[L136] * Select **Add Component**

[L137] 

[L138] To access the components:¬†

[L139] 

[L140] * Click on **Your Temp** from the left side bar

[L141] * Click on **Components**

[L142] 

[L143] You will now be able to add to any design!

[L144] 

[L145] This works with elements or designs you create, whole templates from our library, or single elements from a template.¬†

[L150] 

[L151] New scaling options are available for images in Repeaters. You are now able to select Fit, Zoom, or Stretch to have it better fit your design.¬†

[L162] ### Background Remover

[L163] 

[L164] Automatically detects and removes backgrounds from images. It currently only supports auto-removal and at present it is not possible to custom erase or restore the background.¬†

[L165] 

[L166] To remove the background:

[L167] 

[L168] * Select **Background Remover** from the tool bar

[L169] * Wait for the **loading icon** to finish

[L170] * Click **Save**

[L171] 

[L172] ¬†Afterwards, it will simply disappear!

[L173] 

[L174] ![bee with loading icon on gray background](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432385847955)![Designer screenshot showing how to use background remover function and image of bee on gray background to a bee no background](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432385848211)

[L176] ### Image Masking

[L177] 

[L178] Our new tool allows you to apply shapes or elements as masks to images.¬†

[L179] 

[L180] * Click on the **Mask** icon from the tool bar

[L181] * Select any **shape** or search for more shapes on the left side menu

[L182] 

[L183] ![Designer screenshot with image of lion cub in grass cut into a circle shape, arrows show how to apply mask](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/41432408670227)

[L184] 

[L185] The mask and the image attach automatically when you select it.¬†

[L186] 

[L187] To edit the placement on the picture, **Edit Mask** detaches the overlaid shape.¬†

[L188] 

[L189] **Apply Mask** reapplies it and groups it back together.¬†

[L190] 

[L191] To delete the shape altogether, select **Remove Mask**.¬†
