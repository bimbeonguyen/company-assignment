[L38] ## Best Practices

[L39] 

[L40] We recommend certain best practices to keep your OptiStick Player running at peak efficiency:

[L41] 

[L42] * Do not put too much pressure on the power cable, as this can cause the Player to get pulled from its HDMI socket.

[L43] * Keep the player in an indoor, air conditioned environment

[L44] * Run the OptiStick in fullscreen mode, or with a 2-3 zone Split Screen.

[L45] 

[L46] By default, an OptiStick Player has an [**Operational Schedule**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/28598173096723-How-To-Create-and-Use-Operational-Schedules-HDMI-CEC-RS-232) built in to ensure it has 8 hours of downtime per day:

[L47] 

[L48] ![operational schedule image](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147917435923)

[L49] 

[L50] If you require 24/7 uptime, more than 3 zones on a split screen, a heavy Website Dashboard (including Tableau or Power BI reports), or plan to use the player in hot or cold environments, consider the [**OptiSigns Pro Player**](https://shop.optisigns.com/products/optisigns-digital-signage-player) or [**ProMax Player**](https://shop.optisigns.com/products/optisigns-promax-signage-player).

[L54] ## The Troubleshooting Page

[L55] 

[L56] Your first stop when running into a problem with the OptiStick Player should be the **Troubleshooting Page.** This is an option on the side menu.

[L57] 

[L58] To access it, press the **Three-Bar button** on your remote to open the side menu of the OptiSigns app. Navigate to **Troubleshooting** under the **Advanced Options** section.

[L59] 

[L60] ![open troubleshooting page optisigns app](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147917438355)

[L61] 

[L62] Now you can view detailed information about the appŌĆÖs status and connectivity to assist with troubleshooting.

[L63] 

[L64] ![troubleshooting page optisigns](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147917440787)

[L65] 

[L66] * **Check Internet Connection**: Verifies whether the device has an active internet connection.

[L67] * **Check Connection to API Services**: Tests the device's connection to OptiSigns services.

[L68] 	+ **Note**: If this check fails, it may be due to a firewall blocking the connection. Refer to our [**Whitelist Article**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047275934) for the required URLs and ports.

[L69] * **Check File Downloading**: Confirms the status of downloadable files (e.g., images, videos) being downloaded to the device.

[L70] * **Network Information**: Displays the current network the device is connected to.

[L71] 	+ **WiFi/Ethernet Details**: Includes IP Address, SSID, Signal Strength, Channel, Connection Type, and MAC Address.

[L72] * **Device Information:**

[L73] 	+ Screen Name, Pairing Code, Screen Resolution, OptiSigns App Version, OptiSigns MDM App Version, OS Version, Manufacturer, Model, Serial Number

[L74] 	+ Heartbeat/Polling Interval: Indicates how frequently the device communicates with OptiSigns servers and the last received signal.

[L75] * **Running Time**: Shows how long the OptiSigns app has been running on the device.

[L76] * **Storage**: Displays used and total storage capacity.

[L77] * **Memory**: Displays used and total memory capacity.

[L78] * **System Time**: Shows the current system time on the device.

[L79] * **System Time Zone**: Displays the time zone configured on the device.

[L80] * **Assigned Content Type**: Indicates the type of content the device is playing (e.g., Asset, Playlist, Schedule).

[L81] * **Assigned Content Name**: Provides the name of the content being displayed.

[L82] * **Device Created Date**: Displays the date the device was activated.

[L83] * **Operational Schedule Assigned**: Shows whether an operational schedule is assigned (**Y/N**).

[L84] * **Mute Status**: Displays the current audio status of the device.

[L85] * **Heavy Content Status**: Indicates whether the device is handling heavy content (e.g., 4+ zones or SplitScreen with 4K video) (**Y/N**). This will usually result in lag.

[L94] 

[L95] |  |  |

[L96] | --- | --- |

[L98] | 2.4 GHz WiFi only | 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz WiFi |

[L102] The OptiStick is not compatible with 802.1x Enterprise networks or WPA3 WiFi security.

[L103] 

[L104] To identify and resolve network issues:

[L105] 

[L106] * Create a mobile hotspot, then have your OptiStick connect with it. A successful connection indicates a problem with your general Wi-Fi connection.

[L107] * Try a different network. You may need to move the device to get connected. A successful connection indicates a problem with your general Wi-Fi connection.

[L108] * Plug in an Ethernet cable to see if it can still connect.

[L118] ### Power Troubleshooting

[L119] 

[L120] One of the most common causes of device instability is not using the provided power adapters and cables.

[L121] 

[L122] If your device has any sort of intermittent power issues, ***please ensure it is not being powered by the USB port on your screen***. The USB port does not provide enough power to the device to keep it running under all conditions.

[L123] 

[L124] In the event there is no available power outlet nearby and the USB port is your only option, keep your content load light - meaning, no videos, no large files, etc.

[L125] 

[L126] ### HDMI & TV Connection Troubleshooting

[L127] 

[L128] If youŌĆÖre having problems connecting your OptiStick to your HDMI port or TV, here are some steps to try:

[L129] 

[L130] * Try a different HDMI port on your screen

[L131] * Try to connect the OptiStick directly to your TV without the HDMI extended

[L132] * Try a different TV or monitor to see if it will work at all

[L133] 

[L134] If none of these work, contact our support team at [support@optisigns.com](mailto:support@optisigns.com).┬Ā

[L167] ### Blank Screen Troubleshooting

[L168] 

[L169] If your device and screen are on, but only displays a black screen:

[L170] 

[L171] * Network issues, [**see above**](#Network).

[L172] * Check to make sure there is a Playlist or Asset assigned to your screen.

[L173] * Make sure your Timezones and Schedules match, including your Operational Schedule and normal schedule.

[L174] * Check your firewall settings, and ensure youŌĆÖve [**Whitelisted OptiSigns IP addresses and ports**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047275934-Whitelist-OptiSigns-IP-addresses-ports).

[L175] * Check your Operational Schedule, and verify its power settings are not set to Off. If an Operation ScheduleŌĆÖs power settings are set to Off, it will remain off during the designed time.

[L176] 

[L177] If the device is still not displaying content after youŌĆÖve checked these, check our [**Content Playback Troubleshooting**](#ContentPlayback) section, then try a **Factory Reset**.

[L210] ### How to Factory Reset the OptiStick

[L211] 

[L212] You can factory reset your OptiStick Player if the system is not functioning properly or the OptiStick system will not load.

[L213] 

[L214] There are two ways to do a factory reset.

[L215] 

[L216] #### Soft Reset

[L217] 

[L218] A soft reset can be performed if the system is still accessible and operational. This will erase all the data on the device, and may help improve performance and fix some issues.

[L219] 

[L220] On the Home screen, go to **Settings**. Hit **Device Preferences,** then choose **About**. Finally, choose **Factory Reset.**┬Ā

[L221] 

[L222] ![factory reset settings](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147900617363)

[L223] 

[L224] It will ask if youŌĆÖre sure youŌĆÖd like to perform this function. If you are, hit **OK**. The soft factory reset will take place.

[L225] 

[L226] #### Hard Reset

[L227] 

[L228] When certain Android system files are corrupted, the device will not be able to boot into the Android system. In this case, you will need to perform a hard reset.

[L229] 

[L230] To perform one, youŌĆÖll need a small tool. Think paperclip, needle, or SIM card pin. Then, ensure the device is completely powered off and the USB power cable is unplugged from the device.

[L231] 

[L232] Next, use your small tool to press into the small hole on the side of the device. Hold it, then plug in the power cable while the tool is still pressing the button in the small hole.

[L233] 

[L234] ![optisigns gen 3 player hard reset hole](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147900618259)

[L235] 

[L236] The system will then boot into recovery mode. You can use the button to navigate between selections and will need to press and hold for 1-2 secs to confirm selection. Choose **wipe data/factory reset**, and the factory reset will start.

[L237] 

[L238] ![boot mode wipe data option](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147900620563)

[L239] 

[L240] Then choose **Factory data reset:**

[L241] 

[L242] **![boot mode factory data reset option](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147917447443)**

[L243] 

[L244] Once the process is complete, youŌĆÖll see a **Data Wipe Complete** message at the bottom of the screen. From there, select **Reboot System Now** to finish.

[L245] 

[L246] ![boot mode reboot system now option](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147917449235)

[L247] 

[L248] Your OptiStick will be fully factory reset.

[L252] ## Content Playback Troubleshooting

[L253] 

[L254] This section deals with common issues we see when trying to display content using an OptiStick Player.

[L255] 

[L256] ### Website Display or Sizing Issues

[L257] 

[L258] To display a website as you would see it on a Desktop or PC, change Device Scaling to smallest.

[L259] 

[L260] For more information, see the relevant article on Device Scaling:

[L261] 

[L262] * [**Gen 3 Device Scaling**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/28026588373139-How-to-Fix-The-Scaling-Issue-on-OptiSigns-Android-Device-Gen-3)

[L263] * [**Gen 2 Device Scaling**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/27607906766483-How-to-Fix-The-Scaling-Issue-on-OptiSigns-Android-Device)

[L264] 

[L265] ### Designer or Template Sizing Issues

[L266] 

[L267] If using the [**Designer app**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/4404151402899-How-to-use-OptiSigns-Template-Designer-app-to-make-your-Digital-Signs-in-minutes)**,** try setting the canvas size to 960x540, or change the display size on Android.

[L268] 

[L269] ### Screen Color Distortion

[L270] 

[L271] If your screen is showing distorted colors or displays, try:

[L272] 

[L273] * A different HDMI cable, port, or screen

[L274] * Turning off everything transmitting data between the screen and OptiStick, such as the HDMI-CEC, Dolby Vision, or auto adapting resolution

[L275] 

[L276] ### Fixing the OnHold Warning

[L277] 

[L278] ![device is onhold message](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/40147900625555)

[L279] 

[L280] This warning displays when the device is in the [**OnHold Folder**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500003244381-About-the-OnHold-Devices-Folder). This happens when youŌĆÖve ordered more devices than you had licenses for. Any devices above your license limit will automatically be placed in the OnHold folder and will need to be removed, even when the license limit has been raised.

[L281] 

[L282] ### App Crashing

[L283] 

[L284] To handle crashes, clear the cache and then data of the OptiSigns Player, then reboot. You may need to Factory Reset.

[L285] 

[L286] App crashes are most common when is kept on for extremely long periods of time. To avoid them, schedule a **Restart Timer**.
