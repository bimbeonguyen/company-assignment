[L1] # How to Use the Google Search Console App

[L2] 

[L3] ### In this article, we'll show how to display Google Search Console information on your digital signs with OptiSigns.

[L4] 

[L5] * [What You'll Need](#WhatYouNeed)

[L6] * [Accessing Looker Studio and Linking Google Search Console Data](#AccessingLooker)

[L7] * [Generating Your Dashboard from a Template](#GeneratingDashboard)

[L8] * [Creating a Google Search Console App](#CreatingApp)

[L9] * [Deploying a Google Search Console App](#DeployingApp)

[L10] 

[L11] With the Google Search Console app, it's possible to display near real-time dashboards of information pulled straight from Google Search Console. These dashboards are created in Google Looker Studio and can be customized, then communicated throughout the company via digital signage.

[L12] 

[L13] Let's get started.

[L14] 

[L15] ---

[L16] 

[L17] ## What You'll Need

[L18] 

[L19] * An OptiSigns account - [**Standard Plan** or higher](https://www.optisigns.com/pricing)

[L20] * A Google Search Console account, set up and enabled

[L21] * Access to Looker Studio

[L22] * An [OptiSigns-enabled device](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360021855653-What-hardware-and-devices-are-supported)

[L23] * A screen, [set up and paired with OptiSigns](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/18823504383891-OptiSigns-Getting-Started-Guide)

[L24] 

[L25] ---

[L26] 

[L27] ## Accessing Looker Studio and Linking Google Search Console Data

[L28] 

[L29] In order to get your Google Search Console data into the proper format, you'll need to export it to Looker Studio. Start by going to [lookerstudio.google.com](https://lookerstudio.google.com) and logging in with your Google Account.

[L30] 

[L31] Now, connect your account as a data source by clicking **Create** then **Data source**:

[L32] 

[L33] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942247571)

[L34] 

[L35] Now click **Search Console** as your Connector.

[L36] 

[L37] You'll need to **Authorize** Looker Studio to use your Google Search Console data, set the **Account**, then hit **Connect.**

[L38] 

[L39] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656950572947)

[L40] 

[L41] Once connected, you'll be able to set your data, including which fields and parameters you'd like to include in Looker Studio. Select whichever you'd like to display.

[L42] 

[L43] From here, you have two options:

[L44] 

[L45] 1. Click **Create Report**. This will allow you to create and customize your Google Search Console report from scratch. Do this if you want granular control over how your data looks.

[L46] 2. Use a **Search Console Report** template. This will allow you to quickly organize your data. Use this if you want your data on screen quickly.

[L47] 

[L48] This guide will assume you choose to use a Template. If you choose Create Report, feel free to skip to the next section.

[L49] 

[L50] ---

[L51] 

[L52] ## Generating Your Dashboard from a Template

[L53] 

[L54] Back out to the Looker Studio homepage, then select **Search Console Report**.

[L55] 

[L56] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656950576275)

[L57] 

[L58] This will generate a sample report. Click **Use My Own Data:**

[L59] 

[L60] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942261523)

[L61] 

[L62] You'll need to Authorize the account. Now, choose the **Sites,** then the **Tables**,select the **Search Type**, then hit **Add.** This will fill in one area of the Template.

[L63] 

[L64] Now, set up the report the way you like it. Then, hit **Share**, and make sure the report is set to **Public:**

[L65] 

[L66] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942263187)

[L67] 

[L68] This allows it to be set up, and is necessary for it to display.

[L69] 

[L70] ---

[L71] 

[L72] ## Creating Your Google Search Console App

[L73] 

[L74] Once you have your report set up the way you'd like to display it, click **Edit**:

[L75] 

[L76] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942266387)

[L77] 

[L78] Now go to **File ŌåÆ Embed report**.

[L79] 

[L80] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942267283)

[L81] 

[L82] Now you should be on the **Embed Report** screen. Click **Enable embedding**, then make sure it is displaying an Embed Code. Change your **Width** and **Height** to match the resolution of the display you plan to use as a digital sign - we generally recommend 1920x1080 if you're unsure - then click **Copy to Clipboard.**

[L83] 

[L84] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656950591891)

[L85] 

[L86] Next, go to the OptiSigns portal. Go to **Assets ŌåÆ Add Asset ŌåÆ Apps.**

[L87] 

[L88] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942272659)

[L89] 

[L90] Select **Google Search Console:**

[L91] 

[L92] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942274963)

[L93] 

[L94] Now you can set up your Google Search Console app:

[L95] 

[L96] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942276115)

[L97] 

[L98] * **Name -** The name of your Google Search Console asset. This is for organizational purposes within OptiSigns only and will not display on your screens.

[L99] * **Embed Code** **-** The Embed Code you retrieved a moment ago should be pasted into this field.

[L100] * **Update Interval -** How often OptiSigns will ping your Embed URL to update the data displayed on it. This is measured in seconds.

[L101]

[L104] | **NOTE** |

[L105] | The Preview at this stage may display oddly. This is ok. We recommend pushing your Google Search Console app to a real screen for proper testing. Afterward, we can come back to this and alter the Width and Height elements if they need adjusting. |

[L106] 

[L107] Now, hit **Save**. You'll have created a Google Search Console app.

[L111] ## Deploying a Google Search Console App

[L112] 

[L113] You can deploy your new Google Search Console app as an individual asset, or as part of a [Split Screen](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360026559573-How-to-Create-and-Use-the-Split-Screen-App).

[L114] 

[L115] To get your new Google Search Console asset to a screen, go to the **Screens** tab, then click the screen you want to assign it to.

[L116] 

[L117] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656950606739)

[L118] 

[L119] This brings up the **Edit Screen** tab:

[L120] 

[L121] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656942283283)

[L122] 

[L123] Here, select **Asset** under Content type, then hit **Change** next to Selected Asset.

[L124] 

[L125] Then, select your created Google Search Console Asset:

[L126] 

[L127] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/48656950613011)

[L128] 

[L129] Now hit **Save**. Your Google Search Console asset will now display on screen.┬Ā

[L130] 

[L131] |  |

[L132] | --- |

[L133] | **NOTE** |

[L134] | You may need to alter the Width and Height on the Embed Code to get it to display exactly the way you want it. Be sure to push it to the intended screen first to test it - the Preview sometimes looks odd. |

[L135] 

[L136] You can also deploy it as part of a split screen, allowing you to show other assets at the same time. See how in our [Split Screen app article.](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360026559573-How-to-Create-and-Use-the-Split-Screen-App) It can also be displayed in a Playlist or Schedule.
