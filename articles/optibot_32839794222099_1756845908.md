[L1] # Display Salesforce Dashboards with MFA using Web Scripting

[L2] 

[L3] #### Displaying Salesforce Dashboards on your digital screens is crucial to getting real-time data directly to who needs it. Let's go through how to set up your MFA-protected Salesforce Dashboard by using our Web Scripting app!

[L4] 

[L5] Things You'll Need:

[L6] 

[L7] * [Salesforce Dashboard](https://www.salesforce.com/)

[L8] * [Burp Suite Navigation Recorder](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/burp-suite-navigation-rec/anpapjclbjicacakeoggghfldppbkepg)

[L9] * Authenticator App (ex., Google Authenticator)

[L10] 

[L11] ---

[L12] 

[L13] ## Setting Up MFA

[L14] 

[L15] |  |

[L16] | --- |

[L17] | *If you don't already have MFA set up for your Salesforce account, please visit their support article: **[Multi-Factor Authentication for Salesforce Orgs.](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=sf.security_overview_2fa.htm&type=5)*** |

[L18] 

[L19] Next, go to your account settings > My Personal Information > Advanced User Details

[L20] 

[L21] From there, click **"Connect"** on "**App Registration: One-Time Password Authenticator**"

[L22] 

[L23] ![Salesforce account settings and setting up authenticator app](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/35528791304211)

[L24] 

[L25] When Salesforce prompts you to connect an Authenticator App, **DO NOT** immediately scan the QR code.

[L26] 

[L27] Click "**I Can't Scan the QR Code**".

[L28] 

[L29] ![Salesforce setting up authenticator app. Select 'I can't scan the QR code'](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/35528807336979)

[L30] 

[L31] ┬Ā

[L32] 

[L33] **Copy and paste the alphanumeric string** displayed underneath "Key". **Save this key** somewhere secure, like the Notepad app.┬Ā

[L34] 

[L35] * This is ***necessary*** for the web scripting process later.

[L36] 

[L37] Next, enter that setup key in your authenticator app, then enter the verification code into Salesforce, and connect!

[L38] 

[L39] ![Save the setup key that Salesforce provides you.](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/35528807340691)

[L40] 

[L41] **Your MFA is now set up!**

[L42] 

[L43] ---

[L44] 

[L45] ## Record & Set Up Your Web Scripting App

[L46] 

[L47] |  |

[L48] | --- |

[L49] | *You can refer to our **[How to use the Web Scripting App article](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500012522362)** on how to download Burp Suite Navigation Recorder and record your script.* |

[L50] 

[L51] During the recording process, **write down the exact verification code** you inputted to log in to your account.

[L52] 

[L53] * This is ***necessary*** when setting up the Web Scripting app.

[L54] 

[L55] Once your script is recorded, Burp Suite should automatically copy it to your clipboard. You can also copy it to your clipboard by opening Burp Suite in the extension manager:

[L56] 

[L57] ![Click 'Copy to clipboard' on Burp Suite Navigation extension to copy the script again.](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/35528791311507)

[L58] 

[L59] Log in to your OptiSigns account and open the Files/Assets page to create your asset. Click on **"Apps"** You need to:

[L60] 

[L61] 1. Paste your script into the "**Recorded Script**" box

[L62] 2. Paste the alphanumeric setup key you saved from Salesforce into "**Secret 2FA**"

[L63] 3. Input the *exact* verification code you used during the login process while recording into the "**Recorded 2FA Code**" box.

[L64] 

[L65] ![Web scripting in OptiSigns and pasting the script, setup key, and 2fa code in their respective boxes.](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/35528807348627)

[L66] 

[L67] Click **Save!**

[L68] 

[L69] ---

[L70] 

[L71] Now you can [push this app to your screen,](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/18988049363859) [add it to a split screen app](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360026559573), and more!
