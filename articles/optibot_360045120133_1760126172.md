[L1] # How to use the Wall Street Journal App

[L2] 

[L3] ### In this article, we'll explain how to use The Wall Street Journal app on OptiSigns.

[L4] 

[L5] * [What You'll Need](#WhatYouNeed)

[L6] * [Creating a The Wall Street Journal Asset](#Creating)

[L7] 

[L8] The OptiSigns CBC app allows you to display The Wall Street Journal on your digital signs. It covers the most up-to-date Opinion pieces, World News stories, US Business pieces, Market news, Technology, and Lifestyle pieces.

[L9] 

[L10] QR Codes are displayed, which a viewer can scan with their phone to read the relevant article. The Wall Street Journal can also be displayed as [Scrolling Strip.](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360026559613)

[L16] ## What You'll Need

[L17] 

[L18] * An [**OptiSigns-capable device**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/360021855653-What-hardware-and-devices-are-supported)

[L19] * An OptiSigns [**Free plan or higher**](https://www.optisigns.com/pricing)**\***

[L20] * A screen, [**set up and paired**](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/18823504383891-OptiSigns-Getting-Started-Guide)

[L21] 

[L22] **\*** If using a Free plan, make sure you're using a [Free-plan capable device](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/en-us/articles/33940834613139-What-Do-I-Get-With-an-OptiSigns-Free-Plan)

[L26] ## Creating a The Wall Street Journal Asset

[L27] 

[L28] First, you will need to have your screens set up and paired. For more information on how to do that, click [here](https://www.optisigns.com/blog/how-to-set-up-digital-signs-with-optisigns-and-amazon-fire-tv).

[L29] 

[L30] Then log on to our portal: <http://app.optisigns.com/>

[L31] 

[L32] Go to **Files/Assets**, Click on "App" and add¬†**The Wall Street Journal**¬†on your Account.

[L33] 

[L34] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517575898387)

[L35] 

[L36] ¬†

[L37] 

[L38] Click **The Wall Street Journal**:

[L39] 

[L40] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517616582931)

[L41] 

[L42] Enter your Wall Street Journal App information:

[L43] 

[L44] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517616584211)

[L45] 

[L46] * Name: Name of your Wall Street Journal app, this is the name of the app in your asset list. It will **not** be displayed on your screens.

[L47] * Category: Select the region and category that you want to focus on. You can check as many as you want.

[L48] * Theme: Select Light or Dark Theme.

[L49] * Speed: Select how fast you want the app to scroll or switch between posts. You can also customize your speed if you select Custom.

[L50] 

[L51] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517616589715)

[L52] 

[L53] * Font Size: Set the font size for the Wall Street Journal app.

[L54] 

[L55] Clicking¬†**Advanced**opens up additional options:

[L56] 

[L57] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517575903763)

[L58] 

[L59] * Date Format: Choose from a set of pre-formatted dates, or select a custom date format:  

[L60] ¬†

[L61] 

[L62] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/45517575905171)

[L63] 

[L64] * Scrolling Strip Font Size: If you put the Wall Street Journal app to a SplitScreen zone that is a scrolling strip, you can set how big you want the scrolling text to be.

[L65] 

[L66]   

[L67] After you've configured the Wall Street Journal app to your liking, hit **Save**. Your Wall Street Journal app will appear as an Asset in OptiSigns, and it can now be directly assigned to a screen or used in a Playlist. This asset can then be edited at any time from the Files/Assets tab.  

[L68]
