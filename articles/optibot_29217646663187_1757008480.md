[L19] ## What is OptiSync?

[L20] 

[L21] OptiSync is an integrated solution designed to seamlessly connect with various data sources, including spreadsheets, APIs, and tables.

[L22] 

[L23] **Key Features:**

[L24] 

[L25] * **Easy Setup:** Setting up your data source requires low code or no code, ensuring a quick and straightforward process.

[L26] * **Automatic Updates:** You can link your data source directly to our Designer app, which will automatically update your content.

[L27] * **Real-Time Data:** This ensures your digital display always reflects the latest data, eliminating the need for manual entry, reducing errors, and saving time.

[L28] 

[L29] **Use Cases:**

[L30] 

[L31] OptiSync is ideal for a wide range of use cases, such as:

[L32] 

[L33] * Displaying employee birthdays

[L34] * Restaurant menus

[L35] * Work anniversaries

[L36] * Product catalogs

[L37] * Team introductions

[L38] * And, many more!

[L39] 

[L40] With OptiSync, your digital displays remain accurate and up-to-date, enhancing communication and engagement in various settings.

[L42] ## Adding Your Data Source

[L43] 

[L44] You can add your data source through **Account Settings** or through **Designer app**.

[L45] 

[L46] **Account Settings**

[L47] 

[L48] * Click on your account name in the top right corner

[L49] * Select **More**

[L50] * Select **DataSources**

[L51] * Select **Add DataSource**

[L52] * Choose your data source from the list of options and follow the instructions on how to import it

[L53] 	+ *If you fully open your account settings, it will be under "Advanced" in the column on the left*

[L85] ## Inputting Your Data Source in Designer

[L86] 

[L87] Once your Data Source is set up, you can connect it to the Designer app.

[L88] 

[L89] Go to **DataSource** on the left side of the Side Menu.

[L90] 

[L91] ![datasource location designer](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/42703178733715)

[L92] 

[L93] As previously mentioned, you can add your DataSource here. Or, if you have already created it in the Data Source section of **Advanced**, then it should show up under **Other DataSources**.

[L94] 

[L95] **Select** your data source.

[L96] 

[L97] **Drag and drop** the data source elements onto the Designer canvas.┬Ā

[L98] 

[L99] * You can either drag and drop an entire Row or the individual aspects within the rows.

[L100] 

[L101] A pop-up message will appear, asking "**Would you like to use this data in a Repeater or on its Own?**"

[L102] 

[L103] ![on its own vs repeater gif](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/42703178736787)

[L104] 

[L105] * **Use on its own:** It will be an element on its own and will update automatically based on the data source.

[L106] * **Use in a Repeater:** This will include the data source element in a Repeater component.

[L107] 	+ **Repeater** is a tool that can be used on the Designer application to display and repeat a list of items dynamically.

[L175] ### How to use the Property Mapping Feature

[L176] 

[L177] This advanced feature allows you to change the appearance of specific data in your repeater based on conditions set by the user. These conditions, defined in the data source, are mapped to the corresponding values or elements in the repeater, and the specified formatting is then applied.

[L178] 

[L179] ![](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/42705284279187)

[L180] 

[L181] 1. In Designer, double click on the Repeater that you'd like to change. (This will open the Repeater Editor.)

[L182] 2. Select the element in the Repeater you want to edit, then click **Settings** at the top

[L183] 3. Click **Advanced** **Options** in the Side Menu

[L184] 4. Click **+ Property Mapping** (You'll press this for every new instance of mapping you'd like)

[L185] 5. Clicking on the Property menu, select one of the options from the drop down shown above from the drop down menu or manually type in one of the available options listed below.

[L186] 6. Clicking on the Value menu, selecting the correlating column name (This information is pulled from your DataSource).

[L187] 7. Repeat this as many instances as you'd like for multiple elements within your Repeater.

[L188] 8. Click **Update** to ensure this information is saved and applied to your Repeater.

[L189] 

[L190] #### Here are the listed property mapping options in the advanced settings:

[L191] 

[L192] |  |  |  |

[L193] | --- | --- | --- |

[L194] | Property | What it does | Value |

[L195] | Fill Color | Change the color of a selected shape or text in your Repeater | Accepts a valid **Hex** **color** **value** or **color** name (e.g., #CE657E or Red) |

[L196] | Font Size | Change the size of a text font and how it appears on screens | Accepts numerical value (e.g., 20) |

[L197] | Font Family | Change the font of a selected text, such as Arial, Times New Roman, or custom fonts. | Accepts a string values representing the font family. (e.g., Times New Roman) |

[L198] | Background Color | Change the background color of a selected shape or text in your Repeater. | Accepts a valid **Hex** **color** **value** or **color** name (e.g., #CE657E or Red) |

[L199] | Font Weight | Change the weight or thickness of the text font, making it appear lighter or bolder. | Accepts specific values: normal, bold, lighter, bolder, unset, or 100, 200, 300, 400, 500, 600, 700, 800, 900 |

[L200] | Underline | Specify whether you want the text underlined for emphasis. | Boolean value; accepts true/false or 0/1 |

[L201] | Linethrough | Specify whether you want a line striking through the text. | Boolean value; accepts true/false or 0/1 |

[L202] | Overline | Specify whether the text has an overline. | Boolean value; accepts true/false or 0/1 |

[L203] | Text Align | Change the horizontal alignment of the text.┬Ā | Accepts specific values: left, center, right, justify, justify-left, justify-center, justify-right |

[L204] | Stroke Color | Change the stroke/outline color┬Ā | Accepts a valid Hex color value or color name (e.g., #CE657E or Red) |

[L205] | Stroke Width | Specify the width of the stroke. which determines how thick or thin the outline is.┬Ā | Accepts a number value. |

[L206] | Direction | Set the text direction: left-to-right or right-to-left | Accepts specific values: ltr or rtl |

[L207] | Visible | Determine whether the element is visible or not.┬Ā | Boolean value; accepts true/false or 0/1 |

[L208] | Opacity | Change the transparency level of a selected shape, text, or photo in your Repeater | Accepts a numerical value from **0-1** (e.g., 0.2 is 20%) |
