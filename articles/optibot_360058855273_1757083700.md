[L1] # How to use Google Docs with OptiSigns

[L2] 

[L3] You can set up Google Docs with OptiSigns so that whenever changes are made on Google Docs it will automatically show up on your screen.

[L4] 

[L5] In this guide, we will walk you through the end-to-end process to use Google Docs in OptiSigns.

[L6] 

[L7] ┬Ā

[L8] 

[L9] ---

[L10] 

[L11] ## How to Create a Google Docs App

[L12] 

[L13] **1. Go to our portal: <https://app.optisigns.com/>**

[L14] 

[L15] Open the [**OptiSigns portal**](https://app.optisigns.com/app/screenManagement) ŌåÆ Go to **[Files/Assets page](https://app.optisigns.com/app/assetManagement)**

[L16] 

[L17] Click **Apps** ŌåÆ Search for **Google Docs** and click on the app:

[L18] 

[L19] ![googledocs-3.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/360099047293)

[L20] 

[L21] There are two options to enter your Google Docs.

[L22] 

[L23] * Enter your published Google Docs link directly.

[L24] * Sign in with Google, and select your Google Docs.

[L25] 

[L26] ┬Ā

[L27] 

[L28] ---

[L29] 

[L30] ## Option 1: Enter your published Google Docs link directly

[L31] 

[L32] **Go to your Google Docs**

[L33] 

[L34] Click **File** -> **Publish to the Web**

[L35] 

[L36] ![googledocs-1.jpg](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/39923927688595)

[L37] 

[L38] The "Automatically republish when changes are made" is checked by default.  

[L39] Click **Publish** -> **OK** and you will get a link. Copy that published link.

[L40] 

[L41] ![googledocs-2.jpg](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/39923927694867)

[L42] 

[L43] ŌåÆ Return to your Google Docs app in the OptiSigns portal and fill in the required fields as following:

[L44] 

[L45] ![googledocs-4.jpg](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/39923927695891)

[L46] 

[L47] * Name: This is the name to organize assets, it will not be shown on the screen.

[L48] * URL: This is the Google Docs link that you got from Publish to the Web in your Google Docs.

[L49] 

[L50] ┬Ā

[L51] 

[L52] ---

[L53] 

[L54] ## Option 2: Sign in with Google

[L55] 

[L56] ![mceclip0.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/11413370722707)

[L57] 

[L58] * Name: This is the name to organize assets, it will not be shown on the screen.

[L59] * URL: This is the Google Docs link that is auto-generated.

[L60] * Speed: Select how fast you want the slide to switch between slides. You can also customize your speed if you select Custom.

[L61] 

[L62] **Advanced option:**

[L63] 

[L64] ![mceclip1.png](https://support.optisigns.com/hc/article_attachments/26482312995731)

[L65] 

[L66] * Force Sync Interval: By default, the system will force sync every 12 hours. The minute is 1 hour.

[L67] 

[L68] Click Save.  

[L69] After Saving, you can Preview it.┬Ā

[L70] 

[L71] ### **That's all! Congratulations!**

[L72] 

[L73] You have created your Google Docs asset.

[L74] 

[L75] You can assign the newly created wall to your screen by going to Screens, click Edit screens, and assign the Google Docs to screens that you want.

[L76] 

[L77] You can put the created Google Docs in a Playlist, Schedule too.

[L78]

[L81] | **IMPORTANT NOTE** |

[L82] | Make sure your asset file size **does not exceed 10MB**. If it does, it will only display a black screen. |
