[L1] # Fixing Unexpected Screen Rotation & Incorrect Orientation with OptiSigns Android Stick

[L2] 

[L3] Some Smart TVs have auto-rotation enabled by default, which can also affect devices connected via HDMI, including OptiSigns Android Stick players.

[L4] 

[L5] A few Smart TVs known to have auto-rotation enabled by default include:

[L6] 

[L7] * Samsung Frame TV

[L8] * Samsung Smart TV

[L9] 	+ Neo QLED 4K (QN85B, QN90B, QN95B, QN900B, QN800B, QN700B)

[L10] 

[L11] If you experience unexpected screen rotation or incorrect orientation when using OptiSigns on your TV screen, the issue may be due to the TV’s rotation settings.

[L12] 

[L13] **There are Two Ways to Fix This:**

[L14] 

[L15] 1. 1. **Disable Auto-Rotation in the TV’s Settings**  

[L16] 	

[L17] 		* Open the TV’s Settings Menu

[L18] 		* Go to Display or Screen Settings

[L19] 		* Find “Auto-Rotate” or “Screen Rotation”

[L20] 		* Disable this option

[L21] 	2. **Adjust HDMI Input Settings for OptiSigns Players**

[L22] 		* From the TV’s Home Screen, navigate to the HDMI input where the OptiSigns Player is connected

[L23] 		* Press and hold the Select button

[L24] 		* Choose “Edit”

[L25] 		* Change the device name/type to “PC”

[L26] 

[L27] * +

[L28]
